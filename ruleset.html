1. Introduction & Basic Rules

Hello there, thanks for taking part in our monthly event, the HUB BRAWL. Here’s a guide on how to set everything up correctly and what to do if something goes wrong. Make sure to read through the whole document at least once to gain a more in-depth knowledge.


1.1 Requirements

  • You must be 14 years1 or older
  • You have to live in the corresponding region2
  • You should be able to communicate in English, as this will be the main conversation language between players and the staff
  • Your copy of KnockOut City must be legitimate3
  • You can play on any console and platform that is officially supported (i.e. PC, PlayStation®4 & PlayStation®5, Xbox One & Xbox Series X|S, Nintendo Switch)
  • You must have a Challengermode account and be registered at the corresponding tournaments
  • You must be part of the League Hub Discord server

1.2 Schedule

  • Check-In: 5:15pm CEST (EU) or ET (NA)
  • Start: 6:00pm CEST (EU) or ET (NA)

1.3 Code of Conduct

  • We do not allow any defamatory or offending content within our community
  • This does include, but is not only limited to:
    • Spam messages and NSFW content
    • Discrimination in any way or form
    • Hatespeech, insulting behaviour, verbal abuse and sexual harassment
    • Verbal or physical intimidation and/or threats
    • Publishing any personal details without consent (“doxing”)
    • Advocating or supporting any of the above

2. Setting everything up

2.1 Challengermode

  1. Sign-Up at Challengermode
  2. Set your account up with all the required information
  3. Add your KnockOut City account to your game accounts
  4. Join our Challengermode Space
  5. Create a team or join a team
  6. Join any tournament inside our space that fits your team’s schedule and region

2.2 Discord

  1. Join our Discord server
  2. Read through the server #rules channel
  3. Change your nickname if needed (See 5.)
  4. Don’t forget to assign yourself your region’s role inside the #roles channel
  5. All platform names (Challengermode, Discord nickname, ingame name) have to be the same or clearly related to each other (e.g. Brawler123 = Brawler_123)

3. Tournament System

Before getting started, make sure that you understand the basic tournament system and its characteristics. To prevent any confusion, we defined some crucial terms as the following: rounds are ingame rounds, a game consists of the conventional Bo3 round system and a match is defined as a Bo3/Bo5 series within these games.


3.1 Number of Games

  • Each match until the semi finals will be played in a Bo3 games4structure
  • The semi finals and finals will be played in a Bo5 games5 structure

3.2 Format

  • All games are played in a “Team KO” 3 versus 3 format

3.3 Elimination

  • After losing a match, you will be eliminated from the ongoing tournament (Single Elimination / Direct Elimination)
  • There will be no Loser Bracket / Double Elimination / Second Chance
    (except in the case of a third place / bronze match!)

4. Roasters

4.1 Basics

  • Each player can only be part of one team at a time
  • Team and player names should not be defamatory, offensive, hateful or in any other way violating our code of conduct (1.3)

4.2 Size

  • The minimum size of your roster is 3 players
  • The maximum size of your roster is limited to 4 players
  • Before the start of the tournament, one player has to be chosen as a team captain: This person is mainly responsible for communicating with other players and the tournament staff

4.3 Substitute and External Players

  • If your team consists of more than 3 players, these additional players are considered to be subs6
  • These team members can be swapped with active players before each match, but not in between games or rounds
  • You’re not allowed to alter your roster after a finished and successful registration
  • Bringing in any external players is strictly prohibited
  • You’re not allowed to let another person play on your account7

5. Match Setup

5.1 Seeding

  • The seed will be determined by Challengermode
  • The higher seeded team will create the lobby for each game and is responsible for reporting the results
  • The lower seeded team is responsible for joining on time and verifying the results

5.2 Scheduling

  1. Check who’s your next opponent via Challengermode
  2. Contact your opponent via the Challengermode chat or the #contact-opponents channel
  3. Discuss anything important inside the Challengermode chat or the eu-tournament / #na-tournament channel
  4. If you need help, feel free to contact the tournament staff via the chat or inside the #tournament-support channel
  5. Set the lobby up according to 5.4 and let your opponents join (9.2)
  6. Start the game and play your matches
  7. Report your scores on Challengermode and upload the relevant match media (7.1)

5.3 Match Start

  • Your match begins after you’re assigned a bracket by Challengermode
  • Keep the “No Show Policy” (9.2) in mind

5.4 Spectators

  • Spectators are only allowed if both parties agree
  • Excluded from this are tournament staff members and casters

5.5 Livestreaming Casting & Commentary

  • You are allowed to livestream your matches, as long as you use our suffix “#HubBrawl” in your stream title (e.g. [title of your stream] #HubBrawl)
  • Make sure to mind our code of conduct (1.3) while streaming your matches
  • When your match is being casted, you will be contacted by the caster through the Challengermode chat
  • When your match is being casted, please make sure to be on time

6. Support

In case something goes wrong, feel free to contact our tournament staff. Here’s a guide on how to find and contact them. Try not to contact them in any other way, as this might lead to confusion. Conversations between the staff and players are confidential and must not be posted or shared publicly without proper consent.


6.1 Challengermode Chat

  1. Go to the tournament’s official Challengermode site
  2. Look through the Admin list and find an Admin/Mod that is listed as online (green dot underneath their profile picture)
  3. Contact an Admin or Moderator directly through the “Send message” option
  4. Otherwise, write a message that you need help inside the tournament chat
  5. Describe your problem thoroughly via the chat popup

6.2 Discord

  • Send a message inside of the #tournament-support text channel
  • If verbal discussion is needed, you can join the “Waiting for Help” voice channel and ask to be moved to the “HELP ROOM” voice channel (this should not be the default procedure and should only be used if urgently needed)

7. Statistics Reporting

When you’re finished with your match, it’s crucial to report the correct scores and confirm the results with your opposing team.


7.1 Evidence

  • In order to achieve transparent documentation of the results, every player (especially the team captains) have to screenshot the game results. Be aware that if you do not create a screenshot, you cannot prove your claims!
  • If no team recorded evidence, one team captain may propose a result: This result must then be verified by the opposing team captain

8. Match Interruptions

Sometimes a game can have unforeseen consequences, where both teams can’t really do anything against it. This can be frustrating, but shouldn’t be too big of an obstacle in your way. Make sure to think of these rules, when something unexpected happens. If uncertainty arises, contact the tournament staff.


8.1 Unintentional Interruptions

  • For example: Disconnections, game crashes & spontaneous connection issues
  • The game will be continued (e.g. if one player disconnects during a 3v3 match, the game will be carried through as an 2v3 match)
  • If such interruptions happen in the very first game of your match and both teams agree, the current game can be restarted9
  • The disconnected player is allowed to try to reconnect, but cannot be substituted by another player (specified sub, external player)

8.2 Intentional Interruptions

  • For example: Rage quits & disqualifications
  • The game will be continued (e.g. if one player quits during a 3v3 match, the game will be carried through as an 2v3 match)
  • The disconnected player is not allowed to try to reconnect and cannot be substituted by another player (specified sub, external player)

9. Sanctioning

We’re in for good and fair games. If someone doesn’t obey the rules, insults other players in an arbitrary way or uses malicious third-party software, consequences will be carried through. In each case, whatever the tournament staff decides must be respected and cannot be challenged.


9.1 Verbal Abuse & Harassment

Engaging in competitive sports can be frustrating at times, but we want everyone to enjoy our tournaments. People that harm other people in any defamatory way and/or do not obey the code of conduct (1.3) must expect strict consequences.


9.2 No Show Policy

  • After your match bracket is generated by Challengermode, one team (5.1) has to create a lobby within 10 minutes of the bracket generation and the opposing team has to join within 10 minutes of the lobby creation
  • If this doesn’t happen, please report your situation to the tournament staff (6)
  • If there’s no sign10 from both teams, the tournament staff reserves the right to kick each team from the tournament

9.3 Cheating / Hacking

  • You’re not allowed to use any additional software/hardware, such as rigged controllers, malicious third-party software, etc.
  • You are not allowed to abuse glitches and/or bugs
  • If you witness any of the above described behaviours, please record evidence (in form of video footage, screenshots, etc.) of this person and inform the tournament staff after finishing your match (6)
  • The current bracket will be halted in the case of an active report

9.4 Discord Bans & Brawl Disqualifications

  • There are multiple ways for individuals to get disqualified from the HUB BRAWL (the staff decides the severity in each case):
    • Being disqualified from the ongoing tournament
    • Being permanently banned from taking part in any future HUB BRAWLS
    • Being kicked or banned from the KC League Discord server11
  • Further, there are consequences that can be carried out against a whole team:
    • Being disqualified from the ongoing tournament
    • Being permanently banned from taking part in any future HUB BRAWLS

9.5 Consequences for other teams

  • If a team gets kicked from an ongoing tournament, the following rules will apply:
    • The current or next opposing team gets a free win
    • The previously reported match scores involving this team will remain the same
